## What Is CoinLedger?

CoinLedger (formerly CryptoTrader.Tax) launched back in 2018 when three co-founders — who'd been building automated crypto trading systems — realized that calculating their own taxes was a nightmare. No good tools existed, so they built one.

Fast-forward to today: over **700,000 crypto investors** have used the platform, it's processed more than **$70 billion in transactions**, and it's become an official TurboTax partner. Not bad for a tool born out of pure frustration.

The idea is simple. You connect your wallets and exchanges, CoinLedger pulls in your transaction history automatically, calculates your gains and losses (including staking income, NFTs, DeFi, and everything in between), and spits out a tax report you can actually use. Most users get it done in under 20 minutes.

## A Few Honest Limitations

CoinLedger doesn't accept cryptocurrency as a payment method — you'll need a card. For a crypto-native platform, that's a bit ironic, and it does come up in user feedback.

If you use a niche exchange that isn't in their integration library, you'll be doing some CSV uploads or manual entry. For major exchanges it's seamless; for smaller or newer ones, some manual work may be needed.

And if you're outside the US, Canada, or UK — the supported tax forms and jurisdiction-specific guidance are more limited, though the platform does support international reporting in multiple currencies.
